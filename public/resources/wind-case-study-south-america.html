Executive Summary

A utility-scale wind operator in South America modernized blade health monitoring by replacing manual-heavy workflows with IBIS™ campaign-based external inspections and IRIS™-enabled defect triage. The program improved early-stage defect detection, reduced inspection-to-action cycle time, and lowered unplanned downtime risk.

Customer Challenge

  • Limited visibility into blade-condition progression across a large fleet.
  • Slow handoff from inspection capture to maintenance action.
  • Frequent competition for weather and crew windows.
  • Small defects escalating into higher-cost interventions.

Solution Implemented (Clobotics Workflow)

  1. IBIS™ external capture at scale: autonomous drone inspection campaigns across the fleet with consistent image quality.
  2. IRIS™ defect intelligence: defect labeling/categorization and severity-based prioritization for O&M execution.
  3. Data-to-action loop: IRIS outputs used for planning, stakeholder collaboration, and next-cycle reinspection tracking.

How IBIS™ External Inspections Were Performed (Field Method)

  1. Campaign planning and turbine batching: turbines were grouped by location, historical defect profile, and weather window to maximize daily throughput and minimize turbine-stop coordination overhead.
  2. Pre-flight HSE and mission setup: each mission started with site safety checks, airspace clearance confirmation, and standardized IBIS™ mission templates to keep capture quality consistent.
  3. Autonomous external blade capture: IBIS™ executed automated blade imaging on all three blades with repeatable flight paths, covering key defect-prone zones (LE/TE, pressure/suction side, and tip areas).
  4. Quality control in-field: pilots validated image completeness and clarity before demobilization; missed frames were re-captured in the same weather window where possible.
  5. Defect labeling and categorization in IRIS™: uploaded image sets were reviewed and tagged by defect type/severity to create maintenance-ready outputs rather than raw image dumps.
  6. Maintenance decision workflow: IRIS™ outputs were shared with O&M and technical stakeholders to prioritize corrective actions by severity, accessibility, and outage impact.
  7. Closed-loop verification: follow-up inspections confirmed repair effectiveness and tracked defect progression trends across cycles.
