## 3) Challenge
Before implementation, the customer faced four recurring problems:

1. **Late visibility into blade health**  
   Early-stage defects were not consistently identified fast enough across the fleet.

2. **Slow inspection-to-action cycle**  
   Time lag between data capture, defect review, and maintenance planning reduced response quality.

3. **Resource bottlenecks**  
   Limited weather windows and maintenance resources made prioritization difficult.

4. **Escalation risk**  
   Minor defects could progress and increase outage risk or repair complexity.

---

## 4) Solution: External Inspection Workflow

### A. Fast, repeatable external capture
Autonomous drone inspections were executed in campaign waves to increase turbine coverage with consistent quality.

### B. Standardized defect identification
Captured images were reviewed in a centralized workflow, with defect classification and severity tagging to support actionability.

### C. Maintenance-ready outputs
Findings were structured for O&M planning, enabling teams to align repair priorities with available weather and crew windows.

### D. Closed-loop trend tracking
Defect history and remediation outcomes were tracked cycle-over-cycle to improve planning quality and prevent repeat issues.

---

## 5) How This Supports Earlier Lifecycle Intervention
This model improves intervention timing by:
- identifying defect signals earlier,
- shortening handoff from inspection to action,
- and enabling condition-informed decisions before damage propagation.

In practice, this moves operations from reactive firefighting toward planned, risk-based maintenance.
